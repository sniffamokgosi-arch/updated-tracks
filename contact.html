Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to common questions about our safaris

What is the best time to visit Botswana for a safari?

The dry season (May to October) is generally considered the best time for safaris in Botswana. During this period, wildlife congregates around water sources, making animals easier to spot. The weather is pleasant with little to no rainfall. However, the green season (November to April) offers beautiful landscapes, migratory bird viewing, and lower prices with fewer tourists.

What should I pack for a Botswana safari?

We recommend packing lightweight, neutral-colored clothing (khaki, olive, brown), a warm jacket for early mornings and evenings, comfortable walking shoes, a hat, sunglasses, sunscreen, insect repellent, binoculars, camera equipment, and any personal medications. We provide a detailed packing list upon booking confirmation.

What safety measures do you have in place?

Your safety is our top priority. All our guides are professionally trained and experienced in wilderness first aid. We maintain small group sizes, carry communication devices in remote areas, follow strict park regulations, and conduct comprehensive safety briefings. Our vehicles are well-maintained and equipped with first aid kits and emergency supplies.

What is included in your safari packages?

Our safari packages typically include accommodation, all meals, game drives, park fees, professional guide services, airport transfers, and most activities. Exclusions usually include international flights, travel insurance, visa fees, personal purchases, and gratuities. Each package has a detailed inclusion list on its specific page.

Do you cater to dietary restrictions?

Yes, we can accommodate most dietary restrictions with advance notice. Please inform us of any dietary requirements (vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free, allergies, etc.) when booking. Our camps and lodges are experienced in catering to various dietary needs while maintaining delicious and nutritious meals in the bush.
