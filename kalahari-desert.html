Journey into the Ancient Kalahari

The Kalahari Desert is one of Africa's last true wilderness areas, a vast semi-arid savannah covering much of Botswana. This expedition takes you deep into the Central Kalahari Game Reserve, the world's second largest game reserve, to experience unique desert-adapted wildlife and ancient cultural traditions.

Unlike typical safari destinations, the Kalahari offers a different perspective on African wilderness. Here, wildlife has adapted to survive in harsh conditions, and the landscape transforms with the seasons. Our Kalahari Desert Expedition focuses on walking safaris, cultural interactions with San Bushmen, and experiencing the incredible silence and vastness of the desert.

Walking Safaris

Explore the Kalahari on foot with experienced guides, learning tracking skills and desert ecology.

San Cultural Experience

Visit a San community to learn about their ancient survival skills, traditions, and connection to the land.

Stargazing

Experience incredible night skies with some of Africa's best stargazing away from light pollution.

Desert Photography

Capture stunning landscapes, unique wildlife, and dramatic desert light conditions.

Arrival & Transfer to Kalahari
Maun → Central Kalahari

Arrive at Maun International Airport where you'll be met by your TRACKS guide. After a briefing and lunch, begin your journey into the Kalahari. Arrive at your first camp in time for sunset, enjoying your first taste of the desert's vastness and silence.

Desert Exploration & Wildlife Tracking
Central Kalahari

Morning walking safari focusing on tracking skills and desert ecology. Learn to identify animal tracks and signs. Afternoon game drive in search of desert-adapted wildlife including gemsbok, springbok, and the famous Kalahari lions. Evening around the campfire under spectacular stars.

San Cultural Experience
Central Kalahari

Visit a San Bushmen community to learn about their ancient culture and survival skills. Participate in traditional activities such as fire-making, tracking, and identifying medicinal plants. Experience their unique click language and traditional dances. Evening discussion about San culture and history.

Deception Valley & Predator Search
Central Kalahari

Full day exploring Deception Valley, made famous by Mark and Delia Owens' research. Search for predators including lions, cheetahs, and brown hyenas. Visit fossil river beds and learn about the geological history of the Kalahari. Sunset photography session capturing the desert's changing colors.

Wildlife Viewing
Photography

Salt Pans & Stargazing
Central Kalahari

Morning visit to ancient salt pans, exploring this unique ecosystem and its specialized flora. Afternoon at leisure with optional guided walk. Evening stargazing session with telescope, learning about southern hemisphere constellations. Farewell dinner featuring traditional Botswana cuisine.

Early morning game drive with breakfast in the bush. Transfer back to Maun, arriving by early afternoon. Lunch at a local restaurant before transferring to Maun Airport for your onward flight, taking with you unforgettable memories of the Kalahari Desert.

Kalahari Wildlife

Encounter the unique desert-adapted wildlife of the Kalahari

Kalahari Lions

Famous black-maned lions, larger and more adapted to desert life than their savannah relatives.

Gemsbok (Oryx)

Iconic desert antelope with striking black and white markings and long, straight horns.

Springbok

Graceful antelope known for their characteristic "pronking" (jumping) behavior.

Cheetahs

Desert-adapted cheetahs that thrive in the open spaces of the Kalahari.
