Chobe Elephant Experience

Home to Africa's largest elephant population

The Elephant Capital of Africa

Chobe National Park is renowned for having the highest concentration of elephants in Africa. During the dry season, herds of up to 500 elephants can be seen gathering along the Chobe River, creating one of Africa's most spectacular wildlife spectacles.

Our expert guides will help you understand elephant behavior, social structures, and conservation efforts. You'll witness elephants of all ages - from playful calves to massive bulls - as they swim across the river, feed on riverine vegetation, and interact in their family groups.

The Chobe elephants are known for their impressive tusks, with some bulls carrying ivory weighing over 50kg per side. This genetic trait makes Chobe one of the best places to see truly large-tusked elephants in Africa today.

Safari Activities

Experience Chobe from different perspectives

River Cruises

Relaxing boat safaris along the Chobe River offer unparalleled wildlife viewing from the water. Get close to elephants bathing, hippos yawning, and crocodiles basking on the banks. Sunset cruises with drinks included.

Game Drives

Morning and afternoon game drives in open 4x4 vehicles with experienced guides. Search for the Big Five and other wildlife in different habitats including floodplains, woodlands, and the famous Savuti Marsh area.

Photography Safaris

Specialized photography drives with guides who understand lighting and positioning for optimal wildlife photography. Focus on elephants, birds, landscapes, and predator action during golden hour.

Bird Watching

Chobe is a birder's paradise with over 450 species. Look for African skimmers, carmine bee-eaters, fish eagles, and many waterbirds. Specialist birding guides available on request.
